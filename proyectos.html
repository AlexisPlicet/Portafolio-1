Here I present my most significant project in my professional career.

Electronic E Visa for Seafarers

I participated in the development and implementation of the Electronic E Visa process for seafarers, technicians, and superintendents entering Panama to embark, disembark, or carry out crew changes within national maritime operations. This project emerged as a response to the global need to streamline, digitalize, and secure specialized migration procedures, in compliance with guidelines established by the International Maritime Organization (IMO).

My involvement focused on optimizing migration workflows and transitioning from manual procedures to a fully digital system. This included reviewing, validating, and transforming internal processes to ensure the platform met required security, traceability, and efficiency standards for maritime migration control.

The Electronic E Visa significantly reduced paper usage, improved the accuracy of recorded information, and strengthened document traceability at every stage of the process.

The technological component of the project enabled automated verifications at international airports prior to boarding, connecting with global security watchlists and advanced verification systems. This ensured a faster, safer, and more reliable process for both maritime agencies and crew members arriving from different parts of the world.

This experience strengthened my skills in analysis, inter-agency coordination, technical documentation, and technological adaptation, consolidating my profile as a professional capable of contributing to complex digital transformation projects.
